BigMarker

BigMarker is an end-to-end webinar and video platform that helps provide an all-in-one solution for businesses to create interactive virtual experiences and hybrid events. It is a browser-based tool, meaning no downloads are necessary, and will work on nearly all devices and OS.

Features
  • Live Streaming: BigMarker allows hosts to live stream their event to an unlimited number of attendees. It supports multiple presenters and the ability to switch between presenters smoothly.
  • Interactive Tools: BigMarker has a range of interactive features to engage attendees, including polls, surveys, Q&As, upvoting, and live chats.
  • Breakout Rooms: These rooms allow attendees to participate in smaller, more personalized discussions.
    Customizable Branding: You can fully customize your event page with your own logo, colors, and images to align with your brand.
  • Marketing Automation: BigMarker comes with integrated email tools that let you send reminders and follow-up emails to attendees. It also has built-in SEO tools to help your event get found online.
    Integrations: BigMarker integrates with a variety of popular software, including CRMs, marketing automation platforms, and payment gateways.
  • Analytics: BigMarker gives you insights into attendee behavior and engagement levels during your event. You can see how many people attended, how long they stayed, and how they interacted during the event.
Advantages
  • User-friendly and intuitive to use, making it accessible to a wide range of users.
  • Offers stable and high-quality video conferencing.
  • Allows for a variety of meeting formats, including one-on-one, group, webinar, and hybrid class settings.
  • The breakout room feature is particularly beneficial for hybrid learning environments, enabling smaller group discussions.
Disadvantages
  • Costly for small businesses or educational institutions.
  • The interface might be a bit complicated for first-time users.
  • Technical glitches and video quality issues have been reported in some instances.
Pricing

BigMarker offers multiple pricing tiers, starting from $79 per month (billed annually) for 100 attendees, and going up to custom pricing for the enterprise-level that caters to 10,000+ attendees.

Miro

Miro is a highly versatile and feature-rich online collaborative whiteboard platform. It enables teams to collaborate and communicate in real time. You can create, collaborate, or centralize communication across multiple devices (PC, tablets, and smartphones) regardless of where team members are located.

Features
  • Infinite Canvas: Miro’s whiteboard is virtually limitless. You can create as much space as you need for brainstorming, problem-solving, and more.
  • Collaboration: It allows multiple users to work on the same board at the same time, providing tools to draw, write, drag, and drop images, create sticky notes, and much more.
  • Templates: Miro offers a vast library of templates to help with project planning, user story mapping, agile workflows, and other collaborative tasks.
  • Interactive Tools: Miro includes tools for voting, timer, and commenting which can increase engagement during the sessions.
  • Flash Cards: You can create flashcards in Miro using shapes, texts, and images. These can be easily moved and grouped, allowing for interactive learning activities.
  • Integrations: Miro integrates well with several other tools like Slack, Microsoft Teams, Google Drive, and more.
Advantages
  • It provides a visual collaboration environment that can make brainstorming sessions, strategic planning, and learning more interactive and effective.
  • Real-time collaboration functionality in Miro helps teams feel like they’re working together in the same room, which can boost engagement and productivity.
  • Miro supports video, chat, and presentation mode, which can be useful in a webinar or hybrid class scenario.
  • It is flexible and can be used in various ways to suit your needs, whether for agile workflows, design thinking, or interactive learning sessions.
Disadvantages
  • It might have a bit of a learning curve for users who are new to digital whiteboards, given its extensive feature set.
  • The free version has limited features. For instance, you can only create three editable boards with the free plan.
Pricing

Miro’s free plan includes basic features and allows up to three editable boards. Paid plans begin at $8 per member per month (billed annually), and these offer additional features like unlimited boards, private board sharing, custom templates, and advanced attention management.

Stormboard

Stormboard is an online collaborative workspace that brings team brainstorming and organizing to one location in real-time.

Features
  • Sticky Notes: Users can create and place sticky notes on the digital board, enabling brainstorming, planning, and team organizing.
  • Templates: Stormboard offers numerous templates for different purposes such as business strategy, project planning, agile workflows, etc.
  • Real-time Collaboration: Team members can work on the same board concurrently, enabling immediate updates and feedback.
  • Reporting: Stormboard can generate various types of reports, including spreadsheets and PowerPoint presentations, directly from your board.
Advantages
  • Stormboard allows for easy and interactive collaboration, which can help teams in brainstorming, strategizing, and planning processes.
  • The vast selection of templates helps to structure your work for various use-cases.
  • It supports integration with other popular tools such as Microsoft Office 365, Trello, and Jira.
Disadvantages
  • The user interface might take some time to get used to for new users.
  • The free version is quite limited and only allows for five open storms (or projects) at a time.
Pricing

Stormboard operates on a freemium model, with a free version that offers basic functionality. Their paid plans start at $8.33 per user per month and offer more advanced features such as unlimited storms, advanced reporting, and priority support.

Padlet

Padlet is a digital collaboration tool that allows users to create and share content with others. It’s like a virtual bulletin board where people can share thoughts, ideas, and resources.

Features
  • Customizable Wall: Create your own board or ‘wall’ where you can post thoughts, ideas, images, links, files, and more.
  • Collaboration: Invite others to contribute to your Padlet board, enabling collaborative brainstorming, idea sharing, or content curation.
  • Templates: Padlet provides a variety of templates to choose from for various uses, such as brainstorming, mood boards, timelines, and more.
  • Media Support: Padlet supports different types of media, including text, images, videos, links, and documents.
  • Privacy Settings: Boards can be made private, public, or shared with a select group of people.
  • Notifications: Receive notifications when new content is added to a shared Padlet.
  • Export Options: The content of a Padlet can be exported as PDF or CSV.
Advantages
  • Encourages collaboration and interactive learning.
  • User-friendly, with a simple and intuitive interface.
  • Offers a lot of flexibility with what you can post, making it versatile for a wide range of uses.
  • It can be used across different devices and platforms, making it accessible to everyone.
Disadvantages
  • The free version has a limit on the number of Padlets that you can create.
  • For more advanced features and unlimited Padlets, you need to upgrade to a paid plan.
  • Although Padlet is user-friendly, there can be a learning curve for those who are not tech-savvy.
Pricing

Padlet offers a free version with limited features. They also offer a Pro version for individuals and a Backpack version for schools, which include more features and allow for an unlimited number of Padlets. The Pro and Backpack versions are paid plans.

Kahoot

Kahoot is a game-based learning platform that allows educators to create fun learning games in minutes. It is used as an educational technology in schools and other educational institutions.

Features
  • Quiz Creation: Users can create quizzes with embedded images and videos.
  • Real-Time Learning Games: Users can launch a live game with questions displayed on a shared screen, and players answer on their devices.
  • Reports: It provides detailed reports on how participants performed in the game to help assess learning outcomes.
  • Multiple Question Types: Kahoot supports multiple question types including multiple choice, true or false, and type answer.
  • Collaboration: Kahoot allows for collaboration, where users can co-create and share kahoots.
Advantages
  • Improves engagement and motivation among learners through its competitive and game-based environment.
  • Provides a platform for interactive quizzes which can be used for reviewing or assessing students’ understanding.
  • Kahoot’s reports allow for evaluation of participants’ understanding and progress.
  • Supports different learning environments: it can be used for in-person sessions, virtual classes, or hybrid learning.
Disadvantages
  • Kahoot games are time-bound, which may not be suitable for complex topics that require more thinking time.
  • As it is highly dependent on a strong and stable internet connection, connectivity issues can interrupt a game.
  • The free version has certain limitations in terms of the number of participants and quiz customization.
Pricing

Kahoot offers a basic free version for teachers. For more advanced features, such as detailed reports and a higher number of participants, Kahoot offers various premium plans.

FigJam

FigJam is an online whiteboard tool for teams to ideate and brainstorm together. It’s designed by Figma, a company well known for its collaborative interface design tool.

Features
  • Real-Time Collaboration: Multiple users can brainstorm, create, and edit on the same canvas simultaneously.
  • Digital Sticky Notes: Users can create digital sticky notes to jot down ideas and group them for easy organization.
  • Drawing Tools: FigJam provides basic drawing tools for sketching ideas and concepts.
  • Templates: FigJam includes a variety of templates to get started quickly, like flowcharts, diagrams, or mind maps.
  • Timers and Voting: FigJam allows you to add timers to keep your sessions on track and use voting stickers to gather quick feedback.
  • Integrations: FigJam seamlessly integrates with Figma, allowing you to bring in designs from Figma files.
Advantages
  • Facilitates remote collaboration, enabling teams to brainstorm and share ideas regardless of their location.
  • FigJam’s real-time collaboration and interactive features increase engagement during brainstorming or planning sessions.
  • Provides a clean and intuitive interface, making it easy to use even for non-designers.
  • FigJam’s integration with Figma can be particularly useful for design teams.
Disadvantages
  • As of now, FigJam has a relatively limited set of features compared to other whiteboard tools, as it’s quite new in the market.
  • It might require a learning curve for users who are not familiar with Figma or digital whiteboard tools.
  • The free version only allows viewing and commenting. Editing and other advanced features are available in the paid plans.
Pricing

FigJam offers a free tier with basic features. For more advanced features and unlimited editors, there are various paid plans available.

Nearpod

Nearpod is an interactive presentation and assessment platform that can be integrated into existing lesson plans in any subject area. It allows teachers to create engaging lessons by integrating interactive multimedia elements and formative assessments.

Features
  • Interactive Slides: Create presentations that include quizzes, polls, and short answer questions to gauge students’ understanding during the lesson.

  • Collaboration Board: Enable students to collaborate in real time and share ideas on a digital board.
  • Synchronous and Asynchronous Learning: Teach live, synchronized lessons or create self-paced lessons that students can access at any time.
  • Integration with Other Platforms: Nearpod integrates with learning management systems (LMS) like Canvas, Google Classroom, and Schoology.
Advantages
  • Provides teachers with real-time feedback and assessment data to inform instruction and understand students’ comprehension.
  • Nearpod’s interactive lessons can increase student engagement and participation.
  • The platform is versatile, accommodating both in-person and remote learning environments.
  • Nearpod’s extensive library offers pre-made, standards-aligned lessons across all grade levels and subjects.
Disadvantages
  • The free version is fairly limited, and to access most of the platform’s robust features, you’ll need a paid plan.

Wooclap

Wooclap is an interactive platform designed for audience engagement during presentations, lectures, and events. It allows presenters, educators, and speakers to create interactive content such as quizzes, polls, surveys, word clouds, and other types of activities that participants can respond to in real-time using their smartphones, tablets, or laptops.

Features
  • Interactive Activities: Wooclap allows presenters to create a variety of interactive activities, such as multiple-choice quizzes, open-ended questions, word clouds, surveys, and polls. These activities can be integrated seamlessly into presentations and lectures.
  • Real-Time Responses: Participants can respond to the interactive activities in real-time using their smartphones, tablets, or laptops. This immediate feedback helps to engage the audience and provides valuable insights to the presenter.
  • Audience Engagement: Wooclap is designed to increase audience engagement during presentations and lectures. By incorporating interactive elements, presenters can capture the attention of their audience and encourage active participation.
  • Data Analytics: The platform provides real-time data and analytics on participant responses, allowing presenters to gauge the level of understanding, measure knowledge retention, and identify areas where additional explanation or focus may be needed.
  • Seamless Integration: Wooclap can be easily integrated into various presentation tools and learning management systems, making it convenient for presenters and educators to use in their existing workflows.
  • Accessibility: Participants can access Wooclap activities through a web-based interface, eliminating the need for them to download any specific apps or software.
  • Multilingual Support: Wooclap supports multiple languages, making it suitable for international presentations and events.
  • Security and Privacy: The platform prioritizes data security and privacy, ensuring that participant responses and personal information are protected.
  • Customization: Presenters can customize the look and feel of the interactive activities to match their branding or presentation theme.
  • Live Leaderboard: In quiz-based activities, Wooclap provides a live leaderboard, which can add an element of competition and excitement to the session.
Advantages
  • Increased Audience Engagement: Wooclap’s interactive activities keep the audience engaged and active during presentations, lectures, or events. By involving participants in real-time, it helps maintain their attention and focus.
  • Instant Feedback: Presenters receive immediate feedback from participants through the platform. This feedback provides valuable insights into the audience’s understanding, opinions, and preferences.
  • Improved Knowledge Retention: The interactive nature of Wooclap activities enhances learning and knowledge retention. When participants actively respond to questions and quizzes, it reinforces the material being presented.
  • Data-Driven Insights: The platform provides data analytics, giving presenters a clear overview of participant responses. This data can help identify knowledge gaps, popular opinions, and areas for improvement in future presentations.
  • Customizable Content: Wooclap allows presenters to create and customize interactive content to suit their specific needs. Whether it’s a quiz, poll, survey, or open-ended question, the flexibility enables tailored content for different presentations.
  • Ease of Use: The platform is user-friendly, both for presenters and participants. Creating interactive activities and joining sessions is straightforward, requiring no specialized technical knowledge.
  • Compatibility with Various Devices: Wooclap can be accessed through smartphones, tablets, or laptops, making it highly accessible to a wide range of participants.
  • Facilitates Active Learning: With Wooclap, participants actively participate in the learning process, making it a more dynamic and immersive experience compared to traditional one-way presentations.
  • Promotes Interaction and Collaboration: Wooclap fosters interaction between presenters and the audience and encourages collaboration among participants, creating a more inclusive and engaging environment.
  • Enhanced Presentations and Events: By integrating Wooclap’s interactive elements, presentations and events become more memorable, impactful, and enjoyable for the audience.
  • Real-Time Leaderboard (in quizzes): In quiz-based activities, the live leaderboard adds an element of competition and excitement, motivating participants to strive for better results.
  • International Applicability: Wooclap supports multiple languages, making it suitable for events and presentations held in diverse linguistic regions.
Disadvantages
  • Internet Connectivity: Wooclap relies on internet connectivity for both presenters and participants. In situations where internet access is limited or unreliable, the platform may not function optimally.
  • Device Compatibility: While Wooclap is designed to be accessible on various devices, some older or less common devices may have limited compatibility or functionality.
  • Learning Curve for Presenters: While Wooclap is generally user-friendly, presenters who are less tech-savvy might need some time to familiarize themselves with the platform and its features.
  • Participant Device Dependence: To participate in Wooclap activities, audience members must have their own smartphones, tablets, or laptops. If some participants don’t have access to such devices, it could limit their involvement.
  • Data Privacy and Security: As with any online platform, data privacy and security should be a consideration. It’s essential for presenters to ensure that any personal information collected from participants is handled and stored securely.
  • Connection Issues: Participants or presenters may experience connection issues during live events, leading to delays or disruptions in the interactive activities.
  • Potential Distractions: While Wooclap aims to enhance engagement, interactive elements could potentially become distractions if not used strategically.
  • Limited Customization: Although Wooclap allows some level of customization, users might have specific design preferences or advanced customization needs that may not be fully met by the platform.
  • Cost Considerations: Wooclap may have costs associated with certain features or higher usage levels, and this should be taken into account when considering its implementation for large-scale events or educational institutions.
  • Real-Time Limitations: The real-time nature of Wooclap activities means that presenters may need to adapt quickly to unexpected participant responses or data trends, which might be challenging in certain situations.

Wooclap offers free version with limited features.

Flip

Flip is an educational platform and social learning tool developed by Microsoft. It allows teachers and students to create and share short videos called “grids” or “topics.” Each grid is like a discussion forum focused on a specific subject, and within each grid, teachers can post topics or questions for students to respond to with short video recordings.

Features
  • Video Responses: Flipgrid allows students to respond to topics and questions posed by teachers or peers using short video recordings. This feature promotes active engagement, communication skills, and creativity.
  • Grids and Topics: Teachers can create grids for different subjects or classes, and within each grid, they can post specific topics or questions for students to respond to. This structure makes it easy to organize discussions and keep them focused.
  • Social Interaction: Students can view and reply to each other’s video responses, fostering a sense of community and encouraging peer-to-peer learning.
  • Feedback and Moderation: Teachers have control over the content, and they can moderate responses before they are visible to the entire class. This feature ensures a safe and supportive learning environment.
  • Integration with Microsoft Tools: As a product developed by Microsoft, Flipgrid integrates seamlessly with other Microsoft products like Teams and OneNote, making it a valuable addition to the Microsoft Education suite.
  • Customization: Teachers can customize their grids and topics with different themes, backgrounds, and instructions, adding a personal touch to the learning experience.
Advantages
  • Enhanced Engagement: Flipgrid’s video-based format encourages active participation and engagement among students. It provides an opportunity for students to express themselves creatively, which can lead to increased interest and motivation in the learning process.
  • Improved Communication Skills: By responding to topics with video recordings, students have a chance to practice and develop their communication skills, including public speaking and articulating their thoughts effectively.
  • Peer Learning and Collaboration: Flipgrid fosters a sense of community and encourages peer-to-peer learning. Students can view and respond to each other’s video responses, promoting collaborative discussions and knowledge sharing.
  • Reflection and Self-Assessment: Video responses enable students to reflect on their learning progress and self-assess their work. They can review their responses, identify areas for improvement, and actively engage in their own learning journey.
  • Diverse Assessment Methods: Teachers can use Flipgrid to assess student understanding and progress in various ways, such as through verbal explanations, demonstrations, or creative presentations. This allows for a more comprehensive assessment of student learning.
  • Flexibility and Accessibility: Flipgrid can be accessed on various devices, making it convenient for students to participate from anywhere with an internet connection. It accommodates different learning styles and accessibility needs.
  • Safe and Moderated Environment: Teachers have control over the content that students post, and they can moderate responses to ensure a safe and supportive learning environment.
  • Integration with Other Tools: Flipgrid integrates well with other educational platforms, such as Microsoft Teams and OneNote, streamlining the workflow for educators who use these tools.
Disadvantages
  • Internet Access: Flipgrid is an online platform that requires a stable internet connection. Lack of internet access or slow internet speeds can hinder the ability of students to participate fully.
  • Device Compatibility: While Flipgrid can be accessed on various devices, including computers, tablets, and smartphones, some features may be limited on certain devices. It’s essential to ensure that the chosen device is compatible with all the platform’s functionalities.
  • Privacy Concerns: Since Flipgrid involves video recordings and interactions, privacy concerns may arise, especially when dealing with younger students. Teachers and schools need to implement proper privacy measures and obtain appropriate consent before using the platform.
  • Time-Consuming: Watching and evaluating video responses from multiple students can be time-consuming for teachers. Grading and providing meaningful feedback on numerous video submissions may require careful time management.
  • Limited Offline Access: While students can create video responses offline, they need an internet connection to upload and share their submissions. This limitation may impact learners in areas with limited connectivity.
  • Digital Literacy Skills: Some students may face challenges in navigating and using the platform effectively, particularly those who are less familiar with technology or video-based interactions.
  • Educational Fit: While Flipgrid can be a valuable tool in many educational contexts, it may not suit every subject or learning objective. Teachers need to carefully consider how to integrate it effectively into their curriculum.
  • Teacher Training: Implementing Flipgrid effectively requires proper training for educators to utilize its features optimally and ensure a positive learning experience for students.
Pricing

Flip is free to use.
